Who the f*** is Lucky?

Nathalie Crivelli (born in Zurich, 1984) speaks five languages, has a Magna-Cum-Laude Bachelor in Corporate Communication and a Summa-Cum-Laude Master in Management. After work experience in the fields of event-management and finance in her home country Switzerland, she entered the art scene as a nude art model in 2014. Known under the stage name LUCKY, she has been living in Berlin since 2016. In this context, the idea of applying her communication and managerial skills by promoting and selling artwork of her fellow artists was born. At the Freie Universität Berlin, she hence attended courses in art history followed by a further education in art market management. In 2018, she started her business as an art dealer with focus on pop-up exhibitions and Instagram marketing.

The Swiss entrepreneur explains:

„One of the most common statements of established gallerists whom I’ve had the chance to speak with during my study at FU Berlin was to ‘by no means ever open up a gallery!‘ as nowadays it is not considered a profitable business any longer. I am, however, convinced that there are indeed chances for young art dealers - if they adapt to the ongoing change. The world has become mobile and gallerists should adapt to this with timely limited exhibitions at changing hotspots. This is why I strongly believe in the ‘pop-up‘ gallery model: it offers geographical flexibility, reduces high and continuous rents and, by its short period of existence, it also creates a feeling of exclusivity.

Besides that, the world is currently experiencing a boom of start-ups and with that a new flow of liquidity. The main players of this phenomenon are particularly Millenials with high social-media affinity, hence people of my own generation. Many of these young rookies are highly interested in art, but they often perceive its scene as being outdated and elitist. My goal is thus to build bridges to allow this new segment access into the wonderful world of art. And why should I despise the power of social-media by doing so, as many of the older generation of art dealers is still doing? My followers react with enthusiasm when I post content about art on my profile: they show interest and dare to ask questions. Along with my pop-up gallery model, Instagram is therefore a key tool for my exhibition and presentation concept.“
